What we do

Building Independence - Breaking the dependency culture

 See our Vision Statement

Anyone travelling to these areas will be struck by the cheerfullness and positive spirit of so many people here who have literally nothing and are living day by day. However, a dependancy culture has grown up due to aid from first world countries. We seek to give these people a means to help themselves rather than being dependent on handouts.


We want to harness the power of business to bring these people out of poverty. Not a capitalist monster, but a modern social enterprise that cares and educates its staff and uses its profits to support the local economy, paying for teachers, training adults and looking after orphans.

We are setting up a not-for-profit company in Nairobi to be the vehicle to make this happen. This company will have local Kenyan directors as well as UK Directors from LWSE to oversee the business and ensure that it grows swiftly, looks after its staff well and provides support to the community.


LWSE needs to raise finance to set up this business, but once it is operating successfully then it will be local people generating money to help local causes. The distinction over traditional charity giving is that money invested by LWSE will not be simply spent with a limited short term effect, but will be invested in a business that will provide returns many times the size of the original investment over many years.

Our First Businesses

Living Waters Bakery, Kibera
Living Watera Dairy, Kibera

Our initial plan is to start a bakery, a bakery is a profitable business, only flour, yeast and water is needed to produce a premium product, and it is a business that can be easily up-scaled. This bakery will be located in the Makina market, close to Kibera in an area with high footfall. Kibera is the largest slum in Africa, it is one of many around Nairobi and it will be an ideal location to train and develop staff. We will also sell our produce through agents and set up sales points in other local market areas.


The premises will be rented and the rent is very reasonable, but we need funds to buy the baking equipment, ovens, mixers, pans, etc.




This business model has the advantage that it can be expanded by by replicating the initial business in another part of town, but with the advantage that skilled staff will have been trained up ready to run the new shop.


We also plan to set up a Milk ATM. This is an outlet that has a milk dispenser where milk can be purchased in very small quantities at a lower price than local supermarkets. This will provide a useful service to local people as there are no refrigerators on the slum. The premises will also be a sales point for our bakery products.
