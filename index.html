Living Waters Social Enterprises



A new way to encourage, enrich and give hope to the poorest people in Kenya

What we do

Building Independence - Breaking the dependency culture. 
So many people here have literally nothing and are living day by day. We seek to give them a means to help themselves rather than being dependent on handouts.

 See our Vision Statement

We are setting up two projects to harness the power of business to bring these people out of poverty. Firstly, a business, directly overseen by our Trustees: a modern social enterprise that cares and educates its staff and uses its profits to support the local economy, paying for teachers, training adults and looking after orphans.


Secondly we want to support a Micro Enterprise Development scheme, where we provide small loans to allow people to set up small businesses and so allow them to support themselves. Money invested in a micro enterprise scheme is very efficiently used, as when a loan is repaid then it can be offered to another business.


LWSE needs to raise finance to set up the business and support the micro enterprise scheme. Once the business is operating successfully then it will be local people generating money to help local causes.


We will have a not-for-profit company in Nairobi to be the vehicle to manage the business. The company will have local Kenyan directors as well as UK Directors from LWSE to oversee the business and ensure that it grows swiftly, looks after its staff well and provides support to the community.


The Micro Enterprise scheme will be run by a committee formed from local volunteers who have set up a community organisation to manage the fund and overseen by trustees and volunteers from LWSE and other charities that we work with.


 Money invested by LWSE will not be simply spent with a limited short term effect, but will be invested in people and a business that will provide returns many times the size of the original investment over many years: truly, the gift that goes on giving.

Our Business

Living Waters Bakery, Kibera

A social enterprise business where local people living in the slums of Nairobi will generate finance for their neighbourhood

The plan for our first business is to start a bakery: a bakery is a profitable business, only flour, yeast and water is needed to produce a premium product, and it is a business that can be easily up-scaled and offers training opportunities for employees.. This bakery will be located close to Kibera in an area with high footfall: Kibera is the largest slum in Africa, it is one of many around Nairobi and it will be an ideal location to train and develop staff. We will also sell our produce through agents and set up sales points in other local market areas.


The premises will be rented and the rent is very reasonable, but we need funds to buy the baking equipment, ovens, mixers, pans, etc. and to support the business as it starts to trade.


This business model has the advantage that it can be expanded by replicating the initial business in another part of town, but with the advantage that skilled staff will have been trained up ready to run the new shop.


Micro Enterprise Development

This group has set up a community organisation in Kibera to help to set up small businesses. They will issue loans to enable people to buy stock or equipment and will support these businesses with training and advice. They also hold workshops where the businesses can give support to each other. The businesses will repay their loans and the finance will then be available to another business, thus growing the group and changing more lives. This model means that all donations are very efficiently used to change many lives.
We will support them by raising capital and providing training, advice and encouragement.
